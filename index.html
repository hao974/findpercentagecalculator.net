Frequently Asked Questions

What is a percentage and how is it calculated?
A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. It's calculated by dividing the part by the whole and multiplying by 100. For example, if you have 25 out of 100 items, that's 25/100 × 100 = 25%.
How do I calculate percentage increase or decrease?
To calculate percentage change: ((New Value - Original Value) / Original Value) × 100. If the result is positive, it's an increase. If negative, it's a decrease.

Use our basic percentage calculator. Enter the smaller number as "Value" and the larger number as "Total". The result shows what percentage the first number represents of the second.

What's the difference between percentage and percentile?
A percentage is a proportion out of 100, while a percentile indicates the value below which a certain percentage of data falls. For example, scoring in the 90th percentile means you scored better than 90% of test takers.
